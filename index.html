About Me

Currently, I serve as a Postdoctoral Researcher at KAUST, collaborating with Prof. Mohamed-Slim Alouini on next-generation wireless systems, including NTNs and HAPS. My academic foundation was built at IIT Delhi, where I completed my PhD under Prof. Gourab Ghatak. My doctoral work leveraged Stochastic Geometry for the analysis and optimization of wireless and automotive radar networks. In the latter stages of my PhD, I developed a strong interest in learning theory, which led to research on Sharpe ratio optimization using multi-armed bandit algorithms.

Research Interests

My current research interests are in two directions:

  • Stochastic modelling of wireless networks
  • Multi-armed bandits and online learning
